## **1. THE MECHANISM (Section 235)**
Section 235 of the 2017 Constitution establishes the primary legal track for removing elected officials without criminal conviction. It empowers the National Anti-Corruption Commission (NACC) to bypass criminal courts for "serious violations of ethical standards."

## **2. PROCEDURAL FLOW (The Pipeline)**

  * **Trigger:** An allegation of a "serious ethical breach" (often linked to Section 112 reform initiatives).
  * **Inquiry:** NACC conducts a fact-finding mission. If a majority (not less than half) finds grounds, the case splits.
  * **The Supreme Court Track:** Under Section 235 (1), cases of ethical non-compliance are referred directly to the Supreme Court for a ruling.

## **3. CONSTITUTIONAL SANCTIONS**
Unlike criminal law, Section 235 provides for:

  * **Immediate Suspension:** Once the Court accepts the case, the MP must cease duties unless ordered otherwise.
  * **Lifetime Bans:** Conviction results in permanent loss of the right to stand for any election (Section 235, para 4).
  * **Loss of Voting Rights:** A ban of up to 10 years on the right to vote.

## **4. STRUCTURAL FINDING**
Section 235 creates a **Parallel Judiciary**. It allows the state to achieve the terminal exclusion of political actors (Lifetime Bans) using a lower evidentiary threshold ("Ethics") than required for criminal treason or sedition.
